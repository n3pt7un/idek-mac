idek

A peer-to-peer iPhone control surface for macOS.

idek turns your iPhone into a configurable grid of buttons that trigger actions on your Mac — launching apps, sending keyboard shortcuts, controlling media, running scripts, and more. The two apps talk directly over your local network. No account. No telemetry. No server.

Download for macOS Privacy policy

What it does

  • Pairs an iPhone and a Mac over Wi-Fi or wired LAN, with a TLS-pinned, HMAC-authenticated channel.
  • Configurable pages of buttons with icons, multi-state toggles, and chained actions.
  • 22 built-in action types: launch app, keyboard shortcut, media keys, volume, AppleScript, shell, URL, and more.
  • Long-press, hold-to-repeat, and multi-action chains.

System requirements

Mac: macOS 14 (Sonoma) or later, Apple Silicon or Intel.

iPhone companion app: iOS 18 or later. Available separately.

Both devices must be on the same local network.

Install

  1. Download the latest .dmg from the releases page.
  2. Open the DMG and drag Idek.app to /Applications.
  3. Launch it. The first run will prompt for Local Network and Accessibility permissions — both are required for idek to function.
